DR. Norman Borlaug

the man who saved a billion lives

DR. Norman Borlaug, third from the left trains biologists in Mexico on how to increase wheat yield -part of hid life-long war on hunger.

here is a time line of DR.Borlaug's life:

    .
  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, lowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend university fo Minessota, thanks to a depression era program known as "The National Youth Administration"
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save up money, Works in the Civilian conservation corps, helping starving Americans. "I saw how food changed them", he said
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 1938 - Marries wife of 69 years Margreb Gibson. Get laid off due to budget cuts. Inspired by Elvin Charles Stakman, who teaches him how breeding pest-resistent plants
  • 1941 - Tries to enroll in the militry after the Peal Harbord Attack but is rejected. Instead the militry asks his lab to work on waterproof glue, DDT to control malaria, desinfectant and other applied science.
  • 1942 - Receives a Ph.D in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 1944 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from Dupont, leaves behind his pregnant wife, flies to Mexico to head a new plant pathology program. Over the next 16 years his team breeds 6,000 different strains of diseases. Resistent-wheat, including different varieties for each major climate on Earth
